\section*{Equations of Lines}
For the lines described in each exercise:
\begin{clist}
\item Write an equation of the line in point-slope form.
\item Rewrite the equation in slope-intercept form.
\item Rewrite the equation in standard form.
\end{clist}
\begin{smenum}
\mitemxx
{Slope $-\frac{3}{5}$ through $(2,5)$}
{Slope $3$ through $\left(-2,\frac{3}{8}\right)$}
\mitemxx
{Parallel to $y=\frac{3}{5}x-5$ through $(2,-4)$}

\mitemxx
{Perpendicular to $y=\frac{3}{2}x-4$ through $(-1,-1)$}
{Perpendicular to $y=-\frac{1}{3}x+1$ through $(3,3)$}

\section*{Systems of Equations}
Solve each system below by graphing both lines on the same axes and finding the point of intersection. You can use the graph paper on the other side of this sheet, or a separate sheet of graph paper.
\begin{smenum}
\maitemxx
{\displaystyle\begin{System}
y=\frac{1}{2}x+3\\
y=-\frac{3}{2}x+7
\end{System}}
{\displaystyle\begin{System}
2x+3y=12\\
y=-\frac{2}{3}x+4
\end{System}}
\end{smenum}
Solve each system below by substitution or elimination, whichever is easier.
\begin{smenum}
\maitemxx
{\displaystyle\begin{System}
x=\frac{1}{4}y-20\\
-4x-11y=80
\end{System}}
{\displaystyle\begin{System}
10x+4y=8\\
y=-4x+5
\end{System}}
\maitemxx
{\displaystyle\begin{System}
8x+9y=37\\
x+5y=-7
\end{System}}
{\displaystyle\begin{System}
3x-5y=-30\\
4x+7y=42
\end{System}}
\end{smenum}
