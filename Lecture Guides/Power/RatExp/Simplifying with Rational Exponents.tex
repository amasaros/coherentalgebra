\subsection{Simplifying with Rational Exponents} 
When we rewrite a rational power of a variable expression, we will usually use $x^{p/q}=\sqrt[q]{x^p}$.
\begin{example}
Rewrite each expression using radicals and positive integer exponents.
\begin{lpic}{}{5}
\foreach \y/\exp in {1/{x^{2/3}},3/{x^{-3/2}},5/{x^{-3/5}}}
	\regtextright{3.5}{-\y}{$\exp={}$};
\end{lpic}
\end{example}
We can apply the usual rules of exponents to rational exponents.
\begin{example}
Simplify each of the following.
\begin{lpic}{}{6}
\foreach \y/\exp in {1/{\left(x^{2/3}y^{3/4}\right)^{3/5}},
										3/{x^{1/3}x^{1/5}x^{1/2}}}
	\regtextright{5.5}{-\y}{$\exp={}$};
	\fractextright{5.5}{-5}{$\left(\dfrac{x^{1/2}y^{2/3}}{x^{1/3}y^{3/4}}\right)^{-4/5}={}$};
\end{lpic}
\end{example}
