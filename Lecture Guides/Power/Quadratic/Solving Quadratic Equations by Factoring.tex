\subsection{Solving Quadratic Equations by Factoring} 
\begin{definition}[Quadratic Equation]
A \term{quadratic equation} is one in which one or both sides is a quadratic polynomial (and the degree two terms are not equal).
\end{definition}
We know how to solve quadratic equations as \makeblank[1.5in] equations.
\begin{example}
Solve by factoring.
\begin{lpic}{}{5}
\regtextleft{0.25}{-1}{$x^2-7x-18=0$};
\regtextleft{0.25}{-4}{Check:};
\end{lpic}
\end{example}
\noi In the previous example, the equation was given in \makeblank[1.5in] form. The next example shows a quadratic equation given in a different from.
\begin{example}
Solve by factoring.
\begin{lpic}{}{8}
\regtextleft{0.25}{-1}{$(x-4)^2=9$};
\regtextleft{0.25}{-7}{Check:};
\end{lpic}
\end{example}
