\subsection{Vertex as Maximum or Minimum} 
The vertex gives a maximum or minimum value of the quadratic function (depending on whether it's at the top or bottom of the graph).
\begin{itemize*}
\item If the graph opens up ($a>0$) the vertex gives the minimum value of the function.
\item If the graph opens down ($a<0$) the vertex gives the maximum value of the function.
\end{itemize*}
\begin{Example}Let $f(x)=0.003x^2-0.15x+17$.
\begin{enumerate}\setabc
\item Find the vertex of the graph of x.\makeblanknewf\makeblanknewf
\item Does $f$ have a maximum value or a minimum value?\makeblanknewf
\item What is the maximum/minimum value? For what value of $x$ is it obtained?\makeblanknewf
\end{enumerate}
\end{Example}
