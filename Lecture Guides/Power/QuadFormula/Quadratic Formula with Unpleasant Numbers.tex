\subsection{Quadratic Formula with Unpleasant Numbers} 
Quadratic equations often come up in real-life situations that give unpleasant numbers. We should work carefully when using them in the quadratic formula. \emph{When getting a decimal approximation, it's important not to round until the end.}
\begin{example}
The height in meters of a thrown ball $t$ seconds after it is thrown is given by the function
$$h(t)=-4.9t^2+17.3t+0.8$$
Use the quadratic formula to determine at what time(s) the ball is 5 meters off the ground. Give your answer as a decimal approximation, rounded to 2 decimal places.

\end{example}
