\subsection{Rational Powers with Even Numerator} 
When $p$ is even, $x^{p/q}=\left(\sqrt[q]{x}\right)^p$ contains an even power; in eliminating it, we use the \makeblank.
\begin{fact}
When $p$ is an even integer, $q$ is an odd integer, and $X$ is any expression, the equation
$$X^{p/q}=c$$
is equivalent to the two equations
$$X=c^{q/p}
\quad\mbox{and}\quad
X=-c^{q/p}$$
when $c\geq 0$, and has no solution when $c<0$.
\end{fact}
\begin{Example}
Solve each equation.
\begin{lpicvsplit}{2}{8}
\foreach \x/\eqn in {0/{2x^{2/3}+3=21},1/{(3x+1)^{4/5}=16}} {
	\regtextcolc{\x}{-1}{$\eqn$};
	\regtextcoll{\x}{-7}{Check:};
}
\end{lpicvsplit}
\end{Example}
