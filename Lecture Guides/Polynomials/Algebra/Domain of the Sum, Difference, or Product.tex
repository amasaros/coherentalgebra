\subsection{Domains of New Functions} 
For $x$ to be in the domain of the sum, difference, product, or quotient of two functions $f$ and $g$, we need to be able to do three things:
\begin{itemize*}
\item Evaluate $f(x)$
\item Evaluate $g(x)$
\item Perform the appropriate operation (addition, subtraction, multiplication, or division).
\end{itemize*}
We can add, subtract, or multiply \makeblank[0.75in] two numbers, but we can't divide by \makeblanksmall.
\begin{fact}[Domain of Sum, Difference, Product, and Quotient]
For any two functions $f$ and $g$,
\[
\begin{array}{r@{\;=\;}l}
\dom(f+g)&(\dom f)\cap(\dom g)\\
\dom(f-g)&(\dom f)\cap(\dom g)\\
\dom(f\cdot g)&(\dom f)\cap(\dom g)\\
\rule{0pt}{6ex}\dom\left(\frac{f}{g}\right)&(\dom f)\cap(\dom g)\cap\Set{x|g(x)\neq 0}
\end{array}
\]
\end{fact}
