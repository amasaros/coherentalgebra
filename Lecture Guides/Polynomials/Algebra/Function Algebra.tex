\subsection{Function Algebra} 
We have learned one way to combine functions: \makeblank[1.5in]. We can also combine them the way we do numbers.
\begin{definition}[Sum, Difference, Product, Quotient of Functions]
For any functions $f$ and $g$:
\begin{itemize}
\item Their \term{sum} $f+g$ is given by $(f+g)(x)=f(x)+g(x)$
\item Their \term{difference} $f-g$ is given by $(f-g)(x)=f(x)-g(x)$
\item Their \term{product} $f\cdot g$ is given by $(f\cdot g)(x)=f(x)\cdot g(x)$
\item Their \term{quotient} $\frac{f}{g}$ is given by $\left(\frac{f}{g}\right)(x)=\dfrac{f(x)}{g(x)}$
\end{itemize}
\end{definition}
\begin{note}
We may write $fg$ rather than $f\cdot g$. It is important to distinguish between $f\cdot g$ and $f\circ g$ (the composition).
\end{note}
\begin{example}
Let $f(x)=3x^2-5x+2$, $g(x)=2\Abs{x-5}$. Find each of the following:
\begin{lpic}{}{6}
\foreach \y/\fun in {-1/f,-2/g}
	\regtextleft{0}{\y}{$\fun(3)={}$};
\foreach \y/\fun in {-3/f+g,-4/f-g,-5/f\cdot g,-6/\frac{f}{g}}
	\regtextleft{0}{\y}{$\left(\fun\right)(3)={}$};
\end{lpic}
\end{example}
\noi Constructing new functions in this way is referred to as ``function algebra.''
