Polynomials are exactly those functions that can be built from linear and constant functions by addition, subtraction, and multiplication.
\end{theorem}

Studying the arithmetic of polynomial expressions, we discovered the following fact about polynomial functions.
\begin{theorem}[Closure of Polynomials]
The set of polynomial functions is \term{closed} under addition, subtraction, and multiplication. That is, if $P$ and $Q$ are polynomial functions, $P+Q$, $P-Q$, and $P\cdot Q$ are also polynomial functions.
\end{theorem}
