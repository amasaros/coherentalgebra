\subsection{Dividing Rational Expressions} 
We divide rational expressions the same way we do fractions.
\begin{Example}
Divide. Simplify your answer as much as possible.
\begin{lpic}{}{12}
\foreach \y in {1,11} 
	\fractextleft{0.25}{-\y}{$\dfrac{3x^4y-6x^3y-9x^2y}{5x^2-20}\div\dfrac{3x^5y-27x^3y}{5x^2+30x+40}={}$};
\end{lpic}
\end{Example}
We can divide polynomials in this way. Sometimes, the result is itself a polynomial.
\begin{Example}
Divide. Simplify your answer as much as possible.
\begin{lpic}{}{14}
\foreach \y in {1,13} 
	\fractextleft{0.25}{-\y}{$\left(4x^3+16x^2-9x-36\right)\div\left(2x^2+5x-12\right)={}$};
\end{lpic}
\end{Example}
