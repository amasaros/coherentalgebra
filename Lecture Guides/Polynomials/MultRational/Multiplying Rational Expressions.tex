\subsection{Multiplying Rational Expressions} 
We multiply rational expressions the same way we do fractions.
\begin{example}
Multiply. Simplify your answer as much as possible.
\begin{lpic}{}{8}
\foreach \y in {1,7} 
	\fractextleft{0.25}{-\y}{$\dfrac{5x^2-25x}{x^2-3x+2}\cdot\dfrac{x^3-8}{x^2-25}={}$};
\end{lpic}
\end{example}
We can also multiply a rational expression by a polynomial.
\begin{example}
Multiply. Simplify your answer as much as possible.
\begin{lpic}{}{8}
\foreach \y in {1,7} 
	\fractextleft{0.25}{-\y}{$\dfrac{3x+5}{x^3+27}\cdot\left(3x^3-9x^2+27x\right)={}$};
\end{lpic}
\end{example}
