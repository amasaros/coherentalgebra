\subsection{Factoring Trinomials} 
We know how to factor quadratic trinomials. When they have higher degree, we may be able to pretend they're quadratic.
\begin{Example}Fully factor $10x^4-25x^2-60$.
\begin{lpic}{}{11}
\foreach \x/\step in {-1/1. Factor out the GCF,
											-3/2. Factor the polynomial factor,
											-7/3. Factor each new polynomial factor,
											-10/4. Put it all together}
	\regtextleft{0}{\x}{\step};
\end{lpic}
\end{Example}
