\subsection{Exponent Rules (Product and Quotient of Powers)}
We have certain rules that allow us to combine expressions with exponents.
\begin{theorem}[Product of Powers Rule]
For any real number $x$ and positive integers $m$ and $n$,
$$x^mx^n=x^{m+n}$$
\end{theorem}

\end{lpic}
\begin{theorem}[Quotient of Powers Rule]
For any real number $x$ and positive integers $m\neq n$,
$$\mbox{If $m>n$, }\dfrac{x^m}{x^n}=x^{m-n}
\qquad
\mbox{If $m<n$, }\dfrac{x^m}{x^n}=\dfrac{1}{x^{n-m}}$$
\end{theorem}
