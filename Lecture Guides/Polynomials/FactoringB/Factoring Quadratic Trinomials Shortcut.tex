\subsection{Quadratic Trinomials with Leading Coefficient 1} 
In general, when we use the AC Method, the numbers used to split the middle term do not appear directly in the factors. However, look what happens when the leading coefficient is 1.
\begin{example}
Factor using the AC Method.
\begin{lpicsplit}{}{6}
\foreach \x/\exp in {0/{x^2-5x+6},1/{x^2+4xy-12y^2}}
	\regtextleft{0.5+\x*\value{fcols}+\x}{-1}{$\exp={}$};
\end{lpicsplit}
\end{example}
\noindent We notice that the numbers that appear in the factors are \makeblank[1in] as the ones we found in the AC Method. This gives us a shortcut, as long as the leading coefficient (in at least one variable) is 1.
\begin{example}
Determine whether the shortcut for the AC Method can be used. Factor using the shortcut if possible.
\begin{lpicsplit}{}{8}
\foreach \x/\exp in {0/2x^2+5x-3,1/x^2+3x-18}
	\regtextleft{0.5+\x*\value{fcols}+\x}{-1}{$\exp{}$};
\end{lpicsplit}
\end{example}
