\subsection{Special Forms: Perfect Squares} 
To help us to factor, there are certain special products that it is worthwhile to memorize. The first form we memorize is the perfect square of a binomial.\lbr
To see these forms, find each product using the FOIL shortcut.
\begin{lpic}{}{2}
\regtextleft{0.5}{-1}{$(A+B)^2={}$};
\regtextleft{0.5}{-2}{$(A-B)^2={}$};
\end{lpic}
\begin{fact}[Recognizing a Perfect Square]
A trinomial is a \term{perfect square trinomial} when:
\begin{itemize*}
\item Two terms are positive perfect square monomials (\makeblank[1in] a square number, and \makeblank[1in] powers of variables) $+A^2$ and $+B^2$.
\item The third term is $+2AB$ or $-2AB$.
\end{itemize*}
\end{fact}
\begin{Example}
For each polynomial below:
\begin{enumerate*}\setabc
\item Determine whether two terms are perfect squares.
\item Determine whether the trinomial is a perfect square.
\item If it is, factor it as such.
\end{enumerate*}
\begin{lpicsplit}{}{8}
\foreach \x/\exp in {0/4x^4-12x^2+9,1/x^2+8xy+16y^2} {
	\regtextleft{0.5+\x*\value{fcols}+\x}{-1}{$\exp$};
	\foreach \y/\letter in {-2/a,-3/b,-4/c}
		\regtextright{1+\x*\value{fcols}+\x}{\y}{\letter.};
}
\end{lpicsplit}
\begin{lpicsplit}{}{4}
\foreach \x/\exp in {1/x^2-6x-9,0/x^2-5xy+4y^2} {
	\regtextleft{0.5+\x*\value{fcols}+\x}{-1}{$\exp$};
	\foreach \y/\letter in {-2/a,-3/b,-4/c}
		\regtextright{1+\x*\value{fcols}+\x}{\y}{\letter.};
}
\end{lpicsplit}
\end{Example}
