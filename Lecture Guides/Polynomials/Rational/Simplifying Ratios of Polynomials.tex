\subsection{Simplifying Ratios of Polynomials} 
Simplifying with monomials was easy, because they are automatically factored. With polynomials, just like with numbers, we must factor before we can simplify a rational expression.
\begin{Example}
Simplify $\dfrac{2x^3-7x^2+6x}{2x^3-16}$.

To do this, we first need to fully factor the numerator and denominator.
\begin{lpic}{}{4}
\foreach \y/\p in {1/2x^3-7x^2+6x,3/2x^3-16}
	\regtextleft{0}{-\y}{$\p={}$};
\end{lpic}
Then, we rewrite with the numerator and denominator in factored form, and cancel any common factors.
\begin{lpic}{}{2}
\fractextleft{0}{-1}{$\dfrac{2x^3-7x^2+6x}{2x^3-16}={}$};
\end{lpic}
To finish, we may or may not multiply any remaining factors. Often, we will leave our answer in factored form.
\end{Example}
