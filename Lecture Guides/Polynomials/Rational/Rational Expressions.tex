\subsection{Rational Expressions} 
\begin{definition}[Rational Expression]
A \term{rational expression} is a fraction whose numerator and denominator are both polynomials.
\end{definition}
\begin{note}Any polynomial is a rational expression with denominator \makeblanksmall. This is like the relationship between \makeblank[1in] and \makeblank.\end{note}
\begin{example}
Determine whether each of the following are rational expressions.
\begin{enumerate}\setabc
\item $\dfrac{3x^2-5x+7}{2x^2+4x-11}$\makeblanknew
\item $\dfrac{1}{x^2-5x-2}$\makeblanknew
\item $4x^5-3$\makeblanknew
\item $\dfrac{3x^2}{2y}$\makeblanknew
\item $\dfrac{2x+5}{\sqrt{3x-7}}$\makeblanknew
\item $\dfrac{\Abs{x^2+5x+4}}{2x-7}$\makeblanknew
\item $\dfrac{x^2-x^{1.5}+3x}{x^{2.5}-3}$\makeblanknew
\end{enumerate}
\end{example}
