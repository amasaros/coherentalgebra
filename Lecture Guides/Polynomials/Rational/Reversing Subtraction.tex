\subsection{Term Order in Factors} 
Sometimes, factors appear to be common, but the order of terms will be different.
\begin{Example}
Simplify $\dfrac{16+12x-4x^2-3x^3}{9x^3-18x^2-16x+32}$.

To do this, we first need to fully factor the numerator and denominator.
\begin{lpic}{}{8}
\foreach \y/\p in {1/16+12x-4x^2-3x^3,5/9x^3-18x^2-16x+32}
	\regtextleft{0}{-\y}{$\p={}$};
\end{lpic}
Then, we rewrite with the numerator and denominator in factored form.
\begin{lpic}{}{2}
\fractextleft{0}{-1}{$\dfrac{16+12x-4x^2-3x^3}{9x^3-18x^2-16x+32}={}$};
\end{lpic}
We see two pairs of factors that are not \emph{quite} the same.
\begin{itemize}
\item \makeblank[1in] and \makeblank[1in]\makeblanknewf
\item \makeblank[1in] and \makeblank[1in]\makeblanknewf
\end{itemize}
Using this to rewrite:
\begin{lpic}{}{2}
\fractextleft{0}{-1}{$\dfrac{16+12x-4x^2-3x^3}{9x^3-18x^2-16x+32}={}$};
\end{lpic}
\end{Example}
