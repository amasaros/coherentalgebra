\subsection{Polynomial Equations in Standard Form} 
\begin{definition}[Standard Form]
A polynomial equation is said to be in \term{standard form} if it has an unfactored polynomial on one side, and zero on the other.
\end{definition}
Given a polynomial equation in standard form, we first factor the polynomial.
\begin{Example}
Solve $6x^4+14x^3-40x^2=0$.
\begin{lpic}{}{13}
\foreach \x/\step in {-1/Factor $6x^4+14x^3-40x^2$,
											-6/Solve:,
											-10/Check each solution}
	\regtextleft{0}{\x}{\step};
\end{lpic}
\end{Example}
