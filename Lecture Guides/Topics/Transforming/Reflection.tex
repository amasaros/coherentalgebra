\subsection{Reflection and Rotation}
To reflect a point across the $x$-axis, we negate its $y$-coordinate. To reflect across the $y$-axis, we negate the $x$-coordinate. To rotate by 180\degree, we negate both coordinates.
\begin{example}
In the figure below, the graph of:
\begin{itemize}
\item $h(x)$ is obtained by reflecting the graph of $f(x)$ across the $x$-axis
\item $k(x)$ is obtained by reflecting the graph of $f(x)$ across the $y$-axis
\item $l(x)$ is obtained by rotating the graph of $f(x)$ 180\degree\ around the origin
\end{itemize}
Give expressions for $h(x)$, $k(x)$, and $l(x)$ in terms of $f(x)$.

\begin{displaybox}[Reflection and Rotation]
\begin{itemize}
\item If $g(x)=f(-x)$, the graph of $g$ is the same as the graph of $f$, reflected across the \makeblank[1in].
\item If $g(x)=-f(x)$, the graph of $g$ is the same as the graph of $f$, reflected across the \makeblank[1in].
\item If $g(x)=-f(-x)$, the graph of $g$ is the same as the graph of $f$, \emph{rotated} around \makeblank[1in].
\end{itemize}
\end{displaybox}
\begin{note}
The rotation is the \makeblank reflections.
\end{note}
