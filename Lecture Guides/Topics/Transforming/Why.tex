\subsection{Why Transform Functions?} 
Understanding transformations of functions allows us to do two things.
\begin{itemize}
\item Easily understand the graphs of new functions as we encounter them.
\item Transform the graphs of functions to be more easily studied.
\end{itemize}
\begin{example}
Below is the graph of $y=\sqrt{x}$. Sketch the graph of $y=\frac{3}{2}\sqrt{2x-6}-2$.
\begin{icpic}[x=.5\linespace,y=.5\linespace]

\fill[graphend] (0,0) circle (0.2);
\draw[graphend,->,domain=0:17,samples=100] plot (\x,{sqrt(\x)});
