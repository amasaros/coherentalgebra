\subsection{Distance and Angles} 
We classify triangles based on their largest angle.
\begin{definition}[Classification of Triangles]
\begin{itemize} 
\item An \term{obtuse triangle} is one in which the largest angle is \makeblank[1in], that is, larger than a right angle (measures \makeblank[1in] 90\degree).
\item A \term{right triangle} is one in which the largest angle is a \makeblank[1in] angle (measures \makeblank[1in] 90\degree).
\item An \term{acute triangle} is one in which the largest angle is \makeblank[1in], that is, smaller than a right angle (measures \makeblank[1in] 90\degree).
\end{itemize}
\end{definition}
The largest side of a triangle is always opposite the largest angle. Further, for two triangles $\triangle ABC$ and $\triangle A'B'C'$, if all sides are the same length except $\overline{AC}$ is \makeblank[.75in] than $\overline{A'C'}$, then $\angle B$ is \makeblank[.75in] than $\angle B'$ (as shown in this figure):

\draw[geom] (0,0) -- (4,0) -- (60:2) -- cycle;

Using this fact, we can determine the type of triangle by looking at the side lengths and comparing with the pythagorean theorem.
\begin{displaybox}
Let $a$, $b$, and $c$ be the side lengths of a triangle, labeled so that $a\leq b\leq c$ ($c$ is the largest side length). Then:
\begin{itemize}
\item If $c^2<a^2+b^2$ the triangle is \makeblank[1in]
\item If $c^2=a^2+b^2$ the triangle is \makeblank[1in]
\item If $c^2>a^2+b^2$ the triangle is \makeblank[1in]
\end{itemize}
\end{displaybox}
