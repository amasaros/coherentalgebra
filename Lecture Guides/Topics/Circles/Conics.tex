\subsection{Conic Sections} 
\begin{definition}[Conic Sections]
Circles are one of a family of curves called \term{conic sections}. The equation of any conic section has a \emph{standard form}:
\[
Ax^2+By^2+Cx+Dy+Exy+F=0
\]
where all coefficients are integers (if possible) and $A\geq 0$.
\end{definition}
The family of conic sections includes some curves we've met before, or will meet soon.
\begin{itemize}
\item If $A\neq 0$, $B=0$, and $E=0$, we have
\[
Ax^2+Cx+Dy+F=0
\]
If we solve this for $y$, we get \makeblank, which is the equation of a \makeblank[1in].
\end{itemize}
