\subsection{Distance from a Horizontal or Vertical Line} 
Finding a distance from a horizontal or vertical line is significantly easier.
\begin{example}
Find the distance from $(-3,1)$ to the line $x=7$.
\begin{itemize}
\item The line perpendicular to $x=7$ through $(-3,1)$ is \makeblank[1in]
\item This crosses $x=7$ at \blankpair.
\item The distance from $(-3,1)$ to \blankpair is \makeblank[1.5in]
\end{itemize}
\end{example}
We can do this in general, without going through the whole process.
\begin{itemize}
\item The distance from $(x_1,y_1)$ to the \makeblank[1in] line $x=x_0$ is \makeblank[1in]
\item The distance from $(x_1,y_1)$ to the \makeblank[1in] line $y=y_0$ is \makeblank[1in]
\end{itemize}
