\subsection{Equation of a Line} 
Lines in a plane are described by a \makeblank[1in] equation in two variables. This equation can be given in several forms.
\begin{definition}[Slope-Intercept Form]
The \term{slope-intercept form} of the equation of a line is
\[
y=mx+b
\]
where $m$ is the \term{slope} of the line and $b$ ys the \term{$y$-intercept} of the line.
\end{definition}
\begin{definition}[Standard Form]
The \term{standard form} of the equation of a line is
\[
Ax+By=C
\]
where $A$, $B$, and $C$ are integers with no common factor, and $A>0$
\end{definition}
\begin{definition}[Point-Slope Form]
A \term{point-slope form} equation of a line is in the form
\[
y-y_0=m(x-x_0)
\]
where $m$ is the slope and $\left(x_0,y_0\right)$ is any point on the line.
\end{definition}
