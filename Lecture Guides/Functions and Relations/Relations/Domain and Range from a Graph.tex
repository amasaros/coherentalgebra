\subsection{Domain and Range from a Graph} 
Recall:
\begin{definition}[Domain and Range]
The \term{domain} of a relation is the set of all first entries in the pairs. The \term{range} of a relation is the set of all second entries.

For a relation $R$, $\dom R$ denotes the domain of $R$, and $\rng R$ denotes its range.
\end{definition}
In a graph, the first entries are represented as the \makeblanksmall coordinate, and the second entries are represented as the \makeblanksmall coordinate. We can find the domain and range by \term{projecting} \makeblank[1.5in] the graph into the axes.
\begin{example}
The relation $S$ is shown in the graph below. Find its domain and range.
\begin{itemize}
\item $\dom S = \makeblank$
\item $\rng S = \makeblank$
\end{itemize}
\begin{flushright}
\begin{tikzpicture}[x=0.75\linespace,y=0.75\linespace]
\useasboundingbox (-5.5*0.75\linespace,-5.5*0.75\linespace) rectangle (5.5*0.75\linespace,5.5*0.75\linespace-9ex);
\setgraph{5}{5}{5}{5}
\GraphOnes{}{}
\draw[graphend] (-3,2) -- (4,-2);
\draw[dotfull] (-3,2) circle (\dotsize);
\draw[dotempty] (4,-2) circle (\dotsize);
\end{tikzpicture}
\end{flushright}
\end{example}
