\subsection{Factoring an Irreducible Polynomial?}
Here's an an example of an irreducible polynomial.
\[
x^2-6x+10
\]
By completing the square, we see that

So if we allow \makeblank coefficients, this polynomial factors as
\[
x^2-6x+10=\Bigl(\makeblank[1.5in]\Bigr)\Bigl(\makeblank[1.5in]\Bigr)
\]
\begin{itemize}
\item We call a polynomial we can't factor with rational coefficients \makeblank[1in].
\item We call a polynomial we can't factor with real coefficients \makeblank[1in].
\item What do we call a polynomial we can't factor with complex coefficients?
\end{itemize}
\begin{center}
\Huge\bfseries Nothing!
\end{center}
...because there aren't any.
