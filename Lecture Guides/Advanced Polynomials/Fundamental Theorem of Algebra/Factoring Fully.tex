\subsection{Factoring Fully} 
We now have all of our tools for factoring.
\begin{enumerate}
\item Try the basic techniques first---if they work, they're easier.
\item Sometimes we start out knowing an irreducible quadratic factor. (This might come from something about how the polynomial was set up, or some other source.) If so, divide by it.
\item Use Descartes's Rule of Signs to analyze the real roots.
\item Try rational roots until you get down to a quadratic factor, or until you have eliminated all possibilities.
\item If you get down to a quadratic factor, use the $ac$ method, completing the square, or the quadratic formula to find the last two roots, if possible.
\end{enumerate}
\begin{Example}
Find all real roots of the polynomial $x^5-14x^3-26x^2+105x-26$ and factor it fully, given that $x^2+6x+13$ is a factor of the polynomial.
\end{Example}
