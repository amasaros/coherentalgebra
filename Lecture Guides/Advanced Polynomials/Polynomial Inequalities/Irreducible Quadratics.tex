\subsection{Handling Irreducible Quadratics} 
What if a polynomial has irreducible quadratic factor?
\begin{itemize}
\item An irreducible quadratic factor is never \makeblank[.5in] for real numbers $x$---that's the point.
\item So they're either always \makeblank[1in] or always \makeblank[1in].
\item The sign matches the \makeblank.
\end{itemize}
\begin{Example}
Solve the polynomial inequality
\[
(x-4)(x^2-4x+7)(2x+1)>0
\]
\begin{cpic}{}{12}
\regtextleftlp{0.5}{-1}{When $x=0$, $x^2-4x+7={}$};
\regtextleftlp{0.5}{-2}{So $x^2-4x+7$};
\regtextleftlp{7}{-2}{for all real numbers $x$.};
\foreach \x/\lbl in {-6/{$x-4$},-7/{$x^2-4x+7$},-8/{$2x+1$},-10/Product}
	\regtextright{-4.5}{\x}{\lbl};
\regtextleftlp{0.5}{-12}{Solution set:};
\draw[style=axis] (-4.5,-4) -- (4.5,-4);
\foreach \x in {-1.5,1.5} \draw[style=tick] (\x,-3.75) -- (\x,-4.25);
\end{cpic}
\end{Example}
