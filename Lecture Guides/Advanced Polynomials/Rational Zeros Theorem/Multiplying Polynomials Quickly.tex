\subsection{Multiplying Polynomials Quickly} 
In general, multiplying three or more polynomials takes a long time. For example, if we want to multiply:
\[
(2x-3)(3x^2+4x+5)(x-1)
\]
\begin{lpic}{}{8}
\end{lpic}
\begin{itemize}
\item The \emph{middle} terms in this product are hard to find, because they require\linebreak combining like terms.
\item But the \emph{first} and \emph{last} terms are \emph{easy}.
\item The first term is called the \makeblank.
\item The last term is called the \makeblank.
\item To find the leading term, multiply the \makeblank[1.5in] of the factors.
\item To find the constant, multiply the \makeblank[1.5in] of the factors.
\end{itemize}

\[
(2x-5)(3x+4)(x-3)(x^2+x+5)
