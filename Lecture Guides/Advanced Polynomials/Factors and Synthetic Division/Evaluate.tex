\subsection{Using Synthetic Division to Evaluate} 
We can use synthetic division and the remainder theorem to evaluate a polynomial function.
\begin{itemize}
\item The remainder theorem says that if we divide $P(x)$ by \makeblank[1in], the \makeblank is $P(k)$.
\item Synthetic division makes it easy to divide by \makeblank[1in]
\end{itemize}
\begin{Example}Use synthetic division to evaluate $P(-4)$ where
\[
P(x)=2x^4+8x^3-7x^2-23x+20
\]
We will divide $\Bigl(\makeblank[3in]\Bigr)\div\Bigl(\makeblank[1in]\Bigr)$
\begin{cpic}{}{5}
\synthdiv{-6}{-1}{5};
\foreach \x/\lbl in {0/2,1/8,2/-7,3/-23,4/20}
  \regtext{-3.5+2*\x}{-2}{$\lbl$};
\end{cpic}
The remainder is \makeblank[.5in], so $P(-4)=\makeblank[.5in]$
\end{Example}
