\subsection{Structural Issue: Order}
In numbers, the digits must come in order. In order to use the long division algorithm, we must write our polynomials (both divisor and dividend) in descending order.
\begin{example}Divide:
\[
\left(12x^2-21x-2x^3+20\right)\div\left(4-x\right)
\]
\begin{lpic}{}{11}
\regtextleft{0}{-1}{$12x^2-21x-2x^3+16={}$};
\regtextleft{0}{-2}{$4-x={}$};
\divbracket{5}{5}{-3};
\end{lpic}
\end{example}
