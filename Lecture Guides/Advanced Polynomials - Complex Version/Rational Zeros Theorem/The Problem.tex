\subsection{The Problem} 
Consider the polynomial
\[
6x^6+11x^4+23x^2+35
\]
\begin{itemize}
\item $\makeblank[.5in]=35$, so the possible values of \makeblank[.5in] are $\pm 1$, $\pm 5$, $\pm 7$, and $\pm 35$
\item $\makeblank[.5in]=6$, so the possible values of \makeblank[.5in] are $\pm 1$, $\pm 2$, $\pm 3$, and $\pm 6$
\item There are \makeblank[.5in] possible rational roots:
$\pm 1$, $\pm 5$, $\pm 7$, $\pm 35$,
\rrt{1}{2}, \rrt{5}{2}, \rrt{7}{2}, \rrt{35}{2},
\rrt{1}{3}, \rrt{5}{3}, \rrt{7}{3}, \rrt{35}{3},
\rrt{1}{6}, \rrt{5}{6}, \rrt{7}{6}, and \rrt{35}{6}
\item But looking at the polynomial we see that every term is always \makeblank[1.5in]
\item So the equation $6x^6+11x^4+23x^2+35=0$ has \makeblank[1in] real solutions.
\end{itemize}
With other polynomials, it might not be as obvious, and we wouldn't want to have to try \emph{all} the rational roots to found out that \makeblank[1in] of them work.
