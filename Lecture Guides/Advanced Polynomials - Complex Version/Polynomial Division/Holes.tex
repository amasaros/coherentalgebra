\subsection{Structural Issue: Holes}
When we divide numbers, there's a digit in every place. Compare:
\[
2304
\qquad\mbox{vs.}\qquad
2x^3+3x^2+4
\]
In order to make the long division algorithm work, we must fill in missing terms with zeros.
\begin{example}
To divide
\[
\left(8x^3-28x+15\right)\div\left(2x-3\right)
\]
we must first fill in the gaps in the dividend:
\begin{lpic}{}{10}
\regtextright{2.25}{-3}{$2x-3$};
\divbracket{2}{5}{-2};
\writethink[13]{-1}{10}
\end{lpic}
\end{example}
\begin{example}
We might also need to fill in missing terms in the divisor, as in this example.
\[
\left(2x^4-6x^3+11x^2-9x+12\right)\div\left(2x^2+3\right)
\]
\begin{lpic}{}{10}
\divbracket{4}{5}{-2};
\foreach \x/\lbl in {0/2x^4,2/{}-6x^3,4/{}+11x^2,6/{}-9x,8/{}+12}
  \regtextright{6+\x}{-3}{$\lbl$};
\writethink[15]{-1}{10}
\end{lpic}
\end{example}
