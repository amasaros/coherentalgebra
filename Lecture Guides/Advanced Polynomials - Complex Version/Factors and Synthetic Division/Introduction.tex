\subsection{Synthetic Division: Introduction} 
Because of the remainder theorem (among other things), we often want to divide by polynomials in the form $(x-k)$, that is, just the variable minus a number. 
\begin{Example}Divide:
\[
\left(2x^4-13x^3+26x^2-27x+7\right)\div\left(x-4\right)
\]
\begin{lpic}{}{12}
\regtextright{2.25}{-3}{$x-4$};
\foreach \x/\lbl in {0/2x^4,2/{}-13x^3,4/{}+26x^2,6/{}-27x,8/{}+7}
  \regtextright{4+\x}{-3}{$\lbl$};
\divbracket{2}{5}{-2};
\end{lpic}
\end{Example}
There are several problems with this process:
\begin{itemize}
\item It's \emph{long}
\item It takes up a lot of space
\item We are writing the same number over and over
\item It's easy to lose track of signs in subtraction
\end{itemize}
