\subsection{Synthetic Division: Complications} 
There are two things to be aware of with synthetic division.
\begin{itemize}
\item This form of synthetic division only works if the \makeblank is in the form $x-k$.\end{itemize}
Which of these division problems are appropriate for synthetic division?
\[
\left(4x^3-5x^2+7x-3\right)\div\left(2x-4\right)
\]
\makeblank[\textwidth-.25in]
\[
\left(3x^5+8x^2-7x-2\right)\div\left(x^2-3\right)
\]
\makeblank[\textwidth-.25in]
\[
\left(4x^3-5x^2-6x+1\right)\div\left(x+7\right)
\]
\makeblank[\textwidth-.25in]
\begin{itemize}
\item Just like with polynomial division, we must put the terms of the dividend in order and fill in holes.
\end{itemize}

\[
\left(9x^3+16-21x-2x^4\right)\div\left(x-3\right)
\]
