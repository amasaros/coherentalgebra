\subsection{Writing a Polynomial Function From a Graph} 
Given a graph of a polynomial, we can now guess the expression for the polynomial.
\begin{example}
The graph of a polynomial function $f(x)$ is shown. Give an expression for $f(x)$.
\begin{icpic}[x=0.5\linespace,y=0.5\linespace]
\setgraphlarge
\GraphSmall
\draw[graph,domain=-1.39128:3.05362,samples=100] plot (\x,{-2*(\x-2)^2*(\x+1)});
\end{icpic}
\begin{lpic}{}{15}
\regtextleft{1}{-1}{zeros:};
\regtextleft{1}{-2}{multiplicity:};
\regtextright{2*\value{cols}-1}{-2}{(guess)};
\regtextleft{1}{-6}{To find $a$, choose a point on the graph.};
\regtextleft{1}{-12}{To check, choose another point.};
\end{lpic}
We find $f(x)=\makeblank$
\end{example}
